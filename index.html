Post 1: Welcome to my Website

Rube's World

Welcome to Ruben A. Ramirez' World. It's my world, you're just living in it.

So why do I have a site and WHY ON EARTH would you take your sweet time to join me here?

That is an easy answer. I love to write, I love to take pictures and I love to share with the world, but publication sites and social media sites have goals that, although warranted, are kinda lame. Allow me to explain.

Companies Have Their Own Goals

For instance, Medium has a super lame PayWall that causes you to lose readers if you don't put it behind it. This has caused some notable companies to move from Medium, like FreeCodeCamp and HackerNoon. Another example of an overpowering content hub is Instagram. They need to make money, and do it at the cost of the quality of their users. Another company that has shifted to this mindset is Instagram. They are part of the Facebook Terror group, meaning everything is about ads (the number of times I've seen ads that I've searched/messaged/talked [WTF!?] about is too high). I want to have my own words and pictures out there and in MY control, as well as openness for my fellow readers.

Companies Can disappear

We've seen what negative impact can occur when a company or product goes down. *Looking at you, Google Glass* People lose jobs, companies shut down and sometimes, you can lose some sweet memories. *ahem* Vine. These are a bit drastic cases, but what if FB finally gets punished for their inability to handle data and has to close down?!?! Boom, pictures and content gone. What if Medium decides to get purchased by a super newspaper company and no longer allows for freedom of speech? Boom, precious writings gone. I don't want that.
